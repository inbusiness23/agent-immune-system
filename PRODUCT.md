# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Agent builders, Codex and Claude Code power users, Cursor users, open-source maintainers, and teams whose agent instruction files have grown through repeated incident-driven rules. They are technical, skeptical, and usually arrive with a live pain: their agents are slower, more hesitant, or more contradictory than they used to be.

## Product Purpose

Agent Immune System helps users audit and repair long-term agent instruction health. It detects duplicated rules, stale exceptions, autoimmune instructions, and prompt scar tissue, then routes findings into proportional outcomes: tolerate, watch, archive, demote, delete, promote, or regenerate.

Success means a technical viewer quickly understands the diagnosis: their agent rules may be making them slower, and this project gives them an inspectable way to clean those rules without blindly deleting safety guidance.

## Brand Personality

Diagnostic, credible, and a little provocative. The brand should feel like a senior engineer calmly showing why the system is sick, not like a generic AI productivity toy. It can use a sharp hook, but the proof must stay grounded in real files, diffs, outputs, and human approval.

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS explainer animation with floating cards and abstract dashboards.
- Magic AI claims without visible artifacts.
- Enterprise orchestration sprawl that makes the tool look heavy.
- Meme-only naming such as "your rules suck" without a serious workflow behind it.
- Fake IDE surfaces that do not resemble how Codex, Claude Code, Cursor, or a terminal actually feel.

## Design Principles

1. Lead with the pain: "Your agent rules are making you slower."
2. Show the work system, not magic: real chat, files, diffs, terminal output, and benchmark fixture.
3. Keep humans in control: the tool proposes, the user reviews, then applies.
4. Make agnosticism visible: briefly cycle through Claude Code, Codex, Cursor, and terminal use.
5. Turn scar tissue into regeneration: the memorable payoff is cleaner principles replacing accumulated wounds.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA contrast for all meaningful text. Motion should be purposeful and readable on mobile; avoid fast text changes that cannot be scanned. The video should make the main story understandable without audio by using clear on-screen labels, visible commands, and concise outcome frames.
